## Why Rodrigo moved from chemistry into developer tooling

Rodrigo studied chemistry, applied mathematics, and Arabic before earning a
master's degree in analytical chemistry at the University of Pittsburgh.
Scientific research taught him to question assumptions, design good
experiments, and communicate evidence clearly. Building Python tools to
analyze electrochemistry data during his research made him realize he loved
building tools for other researchers and developers more than doing the
bench work itself. That path led through software quality engineering at
Minitab into developer tooling and developer experience work at Posit on
the Positron IDE.

## Rodrigo's professional philosophy

Rodrigo believes the best products earn trust by reducing complexity rather
than hiding it. His motto: helping developers build, adopt, and trust AI.
He is focused on how AI reshapes software development — helping developers
build trustworthy, reproducible workflows that scale from experimentation
to production.

## Rodrigo's work with Project Jupyter and open source

Rodrigo contributes to Project Jupyter and the broader Python ecosystem.
At Posit he worked on notebook workflows and interactive computing in the
Positron IDE. His open source projects include Conda-Lens (Python
environment inspection), Notebook Observatory (a daily census of the public
Jupyter notebook ecosystem), ChemVerify (verification for chemistry notebook
workflows), and Python Environment Doctor (a Claude Code skill for fixing
dependency issues). He presented "25 Years of Interactive Scientific
Computing: From IPython and Jupyter to IDE-Native Notebooks" at SciPy 2026.
