about

Scientific rigor, product instinct

I studied chemistry, applied mathematics, and Arabic before a master's in analytical chemistry. Research taught me to question assumptions, design good experiments, and communicate evidence clearly — habits that led me into developer tooling, open source, and AI.

At Posit, I worked on the quality and developer experience of Positron, a data science IDE for Python and R — shipping production code, improving notebook workflows, and representing the product through talks and technical writing, while contributing to Project Jupyter and the broader Python ecosystem.

Now I'm focused on how AI is reshaping software development: helping developers build trustworthy, reproducible workflows that scale from experimentation to production. What motivates me most is finding friction and turning it into better products.
